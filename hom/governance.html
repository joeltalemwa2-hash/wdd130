How We're Governed

Articles 6 through 11 of our constitution — membership, leadership, meetings, money, amendments, and dissolution.

Article 6 — Membership

Open, accountable, and revocable

  • Open to any individual aged 18 or above who supports the Organization's objectives.
  • Members must conduct themselves with integrity and respect the Organization's values.
  • Membership may be terminated by the executive committee for misconduct or violation of the Constitution.

Article 7

Executive Committee

The Organization is governed by six elected officials, each with a defined role and a two‑year term.

1Chairperson Overall leadership and representation of the Organization.
2Vice Chairperson Assists the Chairperson and acts in their absence.
3Secretary Records all meetings, correspondence, and official documents.
4Treasurer Manages all financial records and funds.
5Mobilizer Engages the community and recruits members and volunteers.
6All Officials Elected by members at a general meeting; serve a term of two (2) years.

Article 8 — Meetings

Regular, quorate, and recorded

The Organization holds a general meeting at least once every month. Emergency meetings may be called by the Chairperson, or upon written request by at least three (3) members.

A quorum consists of at least half of the registered members. Minutes of all meetings are recorded by the Secretary.

Article 9

Finances

How funds are raised, recorded, and withdrawn — built for transparency.

9(a) Fundraising

Funds raised through member contributions, donations from well‑wishers, grants, and fundraising activities.

9(b) Recordkeeping

All income and expenditure is recorded by the Treasurer and presented at each meeting.

9(c) Withdrawal

No funds may be withdrawn without approval of both the Chairperson and Treasurer.

9(d) Inspection

Financial records are open to inspection by any member upon reasonable request.

Articles 10 & 11

Amendments & Dissolution

How the constitution can change, and what happens if the Organization ever ends.

Article 10 — Amendments Two‑thirds majority

This Constitution may be amended by a two‑thirds (2/3) majority vote of members present at a duly convened general meeting. Proposed amendments must be communicated to members at least seven (7) days before the meeting.

Article 11 — Dissolution Assets reassigned

If the Organization is dissolved by a two‑thirds majority decision of members, all remaining assets — after settling debts — shall be donated to another registered community‑based organization with similar objectives.
