Money wisdom
Top tips for student finances
Simple habits that make a big difference over a semester — and a lifetime.
01

Write down every expense

Track every PENNY you spend for one week — transport, food, data, and snacks. You will be surprised where most of your money disappears. Awareness is the first step to control.

02

Use the 50/30/20 rule

Allocate 50% of your allowance to needs (rent, food, transport), 30% to wants (outings, subscriptions), and save at least 20%. Adjust based on your situation.

03

Cook more, eat out less

Buying food at restaurants daily costs 3–5× more than preparing simple meals. A gas cooker and basic groceries can save you tens of thousands per month.

04

Share costs with coursemates

Split Wi-Fi bills, textbooks, and transport with friends. Group buying cuts individual costs significantly and builds a supportive study community.

05

Avoid borrowing for non-essentials

Only borrow money for necessities like tuition or medical needs. Borrowing for airtime, clothes, or entertainment creates a cycle of debt that's hard to escape on a student income.

06

Start saving early, even small amounts

Saving UGX 5,000 per week adds up to over UGX 260,000 in a year. Start tiny. The habit matters more than the amount.

07

Explore small income opportunities

Freelance graphic design, tutoring younger students, selling crafts, or helping with data entry online. A small side income creates a financial cushion and valuable experience.

08

Plan for tuition deadlines in advance

Know when fees are due. Save a portion every month rather than scrambling at deadline. Missing deadlines due to financial unpreparedness delays your academic progress.

Student-Friendly Investments
You don't need millions to start. These options are designed around a Ugandan student's income, risk level, and goals.
📱

Mobile Money Savings (MoMo Save)

Starting from UGX 1,000

MTN MoMo Save and Airtel Money Save let you lock money for a fixed period and earn interest. No bank account needed — just your phone. Perfect for a first investment.

🟢 Low Risk ~8–12% p.a. Min: UGX 1,000
🏦

SACCO Membership

Starting from UGX 10,000/month

Join a Savings and Credit Cooperative (SACCO) at your university or in your area. Save monthly, earn annual dividends, and access affordable loans when needed. Most campus SACCOs accept UGX 10,000/month.

🟢 Low Risk 10–18% p.a. Min: UGX 10,000
🏛️

Uganda Treasury Bills (T-Bills)

Starting from UGX 100,000

Government-backed short-term investments (91, 182, or 364 days). Issued by Bank of Uganda — one of the safest investments available. Great for students with a semester's savings to grow safely.

🟢 Very Low Risk 14–17% p.a. Min: UGX 100,000
💼

Micro Business / Side Hustle

Starting from UGX 20,000

Print & sell revision notes, offer typing services, resell snacks on campus, or provide tutoring. A micro-business earns returns faster than any savings account — and teaches real entrepreneurship.

🟡 Medium Risk Varies 20–200% Min: UGX 20,000
📦

Unit Trusts (Collective Investment)

Starting from UGX 50,000

Providers like Britam, ICEA Lion, and UAP Old Mutual run unit trust funds in Uganda. Pool your money with others, managed by professionals. Higher returns than a bank account, more accessible than the stock market.

🟡 Medium Risk 12–20% p.a. Min: UGX 50,000
📊

Uganda Securities Exchange (USE)

Starting from UGX 500,000

Buy shares in listed Ugandan companies like Stanbic, or MTN Uganda through a licensed broker. Long-term investment — best if you can leave money for 2+ years. High potential, higher risk.

🔴 Higher Risk 15–40%+ p.a. Min: UGX 500,000

💡 Before You Invest
01

Clear high-cost debt first

Any debt charging more than 20% interest should be paid off before investing. You can't out-earn expensive debt.

02

Build an emergency fund first

Keep at least 1 month's expenses liquid before locking money away. Emergencies force you to break investments at a loss.

03

Start small, stay consistent

UGX 5,000 per week invested consistently beats UGX 200,000 invested once. The habit compounds — not just the money.

04

Never invest what you can't afford to lose

Higher returns always come with higher risk. Only put surplus money — not tuition or rent — into medium/high-risk options.

05

Diversify across at least 2 options

Don't put everything in one place. A MoMo Save + a SACCO membership covers safety and decent returns simultaneously.

Our story

Built by a student,
for students.

Taljoe was born out of a real problem — managing money on a tight student budget with no practical tools designed for our reality.

🎯

Our Mission

To empower every Ugandan student with simple, practical financial tools that fit their real income, lifestyle, and goals — making money management a daily habit, not a struggle.

🔭

Our Vision

A generation of financially literate Ugandan graduates who enter the workforce debt-free, savings-first, and equipped to build lasting wealth.

💎

Our Values

Simplicity, honesty, and relevance. We build only what students actually need — no bloat, no jargon, no features that don't apply to life in Uganda.
