Five objectives.
One constitution.

Article 5 of our constitution names exactly what the Organization exists to do. Nothing we run falls outside these five lines.

Objective 5.1

Identify & Support Vulnerable Children

We identify and support vulnerable children with school fees and scholastic materials — keeping kids in class who would otherwise be priced out of an education.

Education Support5.1
HIV/AIDS Programs5.2

Objective 5.2

HIV/AIDS Awareness, Prevention & Support

We promote HIV/AIDS awareness, prevention, and counselling, and provide direct support to affected individuals and families — meeting both the medical and emotional sides of the issue.

Objective 5.3

Community Outreach & Aid

We conduct ongoing community outreach, going directly to where families are rather than waiting for them to come to us, and provide aid to vulnerable households as needs are identified.

Outreach5.3
Income Generation5.4

Objective 5.4

Small Income‑Generating Projects

We initiate small income‑generating projects that promote self‑reliance and reduce poverty — giving families a sustainable path, not just a one‑time handout.

Objective 5.5

Partnerships That Mobilize Resources

We partner with government bodies, NGOs, and well‑wishers to mobilize resources for community development — multiplying what we can do alone.

The work, in practice.

How the five objectives translate into ongoing programs on the ground in Masaka City.

School Fees & Materials

Direct support so vulnerable children can enroll, stay in school, and have the books and supplies they need.

Health Counselling

HIV/AIDS awareness sessions, prevention guidance, and one‑on‑one counselling for affected families.

Household Visits

Field teams visit identified families to assess needs and connect them to the right kind of support.

Livelihood Projects

Small-scale, income-generating activities designed with families to build lasting self-reliance.
