Who we are, and
where we stand.

Articles 1 through 4 of our constitution lay the foundation: our name, where we work, what kind of community we're trying to build, and why.

Article 1 — Name

Homez Community
Development Initiative

The organization is formally known as Homez Community Development Initiative, referred to throughout our constitution simply as "the Organization."

That name carries a deliberate idea: that development starts at home — in the household, the family, the immediate community — before it can scale outward to a city or a country.

Article 2 — Location

Headquartered in Masaka City, reaching outward.

The Organization is headquartered in Masaka City, Uganda. Our day‑to‑day work is rooted here — but our constitution allows operations to extend to surrounding sub‑counties as approved by the executive committee.

That structure keeps us close to the families we serve while leaving room to grow where the need is greatest.

Vision & Mission

Articles 3 and 4 — the two ideas every program at Homez is measured against.

Article 3 — Vision

A Community of Access

A community where every family has access to education, health information, and economic opportunity.

Article 4 — Mission

Empowering Vulnerable Families

To empower vulnerable families and children through education support, HIV/AIDS awareness, and community development programs — the practical, on‑the‑ground expression of our vision.
